# Weak Example: Six of Seven Greens

**Synthetic weak example.** This is intentionally inadequate.

> Readiness is 86% because six of seven workstreams are green. Human approval makes the remaining quality risk acceptable. Recommend launch across all plans and ask legal to finalize the vendor terms during the pilot.

## Why It Fails

- An aggregate percentage hides non-compensable gaps.
- The test population did not represent all plans, languages, or document types.
- Material omissions had no predetermined acceptance threshold.
- Human review is treated as a control without evidence that it works under workload.
- Unresolved privacy, security, legal, and domain decisions are treated as paperwork.
- No accountable business owner accepts the decision.
- There is no incident playbook, monitoring plan, exercised fallback, or rollback.
- The recommendation has no constrained population, expiry, stop condition, or re-review trigger.
- It confuses a financial forecast with demonstrated net value.

## Minimum Repair

Profile each readiness area separately, surface critical gaps before any summary, reserve specialist decisions, name the permitted interim motion, and require owners, monitoring, expiry, and evidence conditions before reconsidering live operation.
