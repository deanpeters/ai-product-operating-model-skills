# Weak Example: Savings Wins

**Synthetic weak example.** This is intentionally inadequate.

> The appeal assistant scores 9/10 for impact because it saves ten minutes per case and $900,000 annually. Feasibility is 8/10 because humans approve every decision. Fund the pilot immediately and resolve privacy during implementation.

## Why It Fails

- It extrapolates one employer plan to the full business.
- It treats a forecast as demonstrated economics.
- Human approval is assumed effective without workload evidence.
- Three material omissions disappear inside a numeric score.
- The test had no predetermined quality threshold.
- Privacy and security decisions are postponed even though vendor retention may constrain the test itself.
- No current-workflow or non-AI alternative is compared.
- It confuses a triage posture with live-pilot readiness.
- No accountable business owner, next test, stop rule, or review trigger is named.

## Minimum Repair

Recommend a bounded validation posture, preserve specialist authority, compare the proposal with the current workflow, define representative cases and thresholds before testing, and route any later live-pilot decision through a readiness review.
