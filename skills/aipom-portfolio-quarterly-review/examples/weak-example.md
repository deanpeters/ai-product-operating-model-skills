# Weak Example: Traffic-Light Portfolio

**Synthetic weak example.** This is intentionally inadequate.

> All six pilots are on track. Three are green, two amber, and one red. Leadership asked every team to accelerate adoption next quarter. Existing pilots do not count against the two new investment slots because they are already funded. Exceptions will be reviewed later.

## Why It Fails

- Colors replace comparable evidence and explicit decisions.
- Discovery, validation, and production work are judged as though they need the same proof.
- Existing commitments are excluded from scarcity without an authorized capacity rule.
- No initiative is constrained, paused, stopped, or retired.
- Shared dependencies and mandatory safeguards are invisible.
- Exceptions have no authority, scope, safeguards, expiry, or renewal evidence.
- Adoption is recommended without outcome, readiness, control, or production evidence.
- No capacity is actually reallocated.
- Owners, dissent, communication, and next-review evidence are absent.

## Minimum Repair

Define usable capacity units, normalize evidence by stage and consequence, apply critical-gap logic, make an explicit decision for every material initiative, fund shared conditions where warranted, and record allocation assumptions and exception expiry.
