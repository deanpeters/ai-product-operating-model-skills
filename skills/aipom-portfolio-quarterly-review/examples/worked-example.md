# Synthetic Worked Example: Support Portfolio Q3

**Synthetic example.** The organization, initiatives, evidence, and allocations below are fictional.

## Review Contract

The portfolio has capacity for two material investments this quarter. Continuing constrained production and funding remediation consumes one slot; a small closure motion does not. The portfolio council owns capital allocation. Any exception to a failed threshold requires the support executive and risk authority, expires at the next review, and cannot authorize scope expansion.

## Initiative Decisions

| Initiative | Stage and evidence | Critical gap or dependency | Decision | Allocation | Owner and next evidence |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| Support case briefs | Limited production; preparation time is 18% lower and manual fallback works | Critical-fact omission is 1.8% against a 1% threshold | **Constrain and remediate**; retain human review and current scope | Material slot 1 | Support VP; demonstrate omission at or below threshold on a representative sample |
| Autonomous refunds | Validation; savings are forecast | Rollback is untested and authority above $500 is unresolved | **Pause** autonomous operation | No material allocation | Operations owner must establish authority and exercise rollback before re-entry |
| Research tagging | Pilot; 12% usage, two failed learning milestones | Owner disappeared after reorganization | **Stop and retire** | Closure capacity only | Product Operations records learning and releases capacity |
| Shared evaluation service | Shared capability for three initiatives | Two engineers required; adoption and savings are not yet quantified | **Fund conditionally for one quarter** | Material slot 2 | Evaluation-platform owner; show integrations, reuse, avoided duplicate setup, and decision impact |

## Shared Operating Investment

The shared evaluation service receives a material slot because it can improve several strategically relevant bets, beginning with the support-brief omission threshold. Funding is conditional on a named owner and delivery plan. Renewal requires demonstrated reuse and better evaluation decisions, not merely a service launch.

## Exception Record

The support-brief exception permits only continued operation in the existing bounded scope. It requires human review, working fallback, daily incident tracking, and no expansion. It expires at the next quarterly review unless the accountable authorities explicitly renew it using current evidence.

Autonomous refunds receive no exception. Forecast savings cannot compensate for untested rollback or unresolved authority.

## Portfolio Learning

- Shift the near-term posture from broad autonomy toward evidence-backed assistance with human accountability and reliable fallback.
- Treat shared evaluation infrastructure as a visible portfolio investment rather than hidden overhead.
- Treat stopping research tagging as functioning governance: repeated failed learning milestones and absent ownership should release capacity.
- Retire the assumptions that forecast savings prove autonomy readiness and that a pilot deserves continuation merely because it exists.

## Communication and Next Review

Communicate the two funded choices, support constraint, refund pause, and research-tagging closure to initiative owners and affected teams. The next review examines:

- Support omission rate, sample representativeness, fallback effectiveness, and exception expiry
- Shared-service integrations, reuse, setup reduction, and contribution to decisions
- Refund authority, rollback results, and controlled economics
- Research-tagging closure, retained learning, and released capacity

The capacity interpretation is explicit in this example. If the council had not defined whether continuing work consumed a material slot, the facilitator would record that as an unresolved allocation assumption before recommending an allocation.
