# Synthetic Worked Example: Support Escalation Triage

A software company observes that specialist support engineers spend 18% of weekly capacity re-reading long account histories before deciding whether to accept escalations. The frame distinguishes measured preparation time from the unverified belief that summaries improve resolution. It compares better intake forms, routing rules, search, and AI-generated case briefs. AI may help synthesize uneven histories, but could hide contradictory evidence or sensitive notes. The next test uses 30 historical cases to compare preparation time, missing-critical-fact rate, and reviewer confidence; the support operations director owns the decision to proceed.

This is strong because it names evidence, alternatives, failure consequences, uncertainty, and the next decision without promising an AI solution.
