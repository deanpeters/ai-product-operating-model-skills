# Worked Example

**Synthetic example.** A recruiting assistant ranks candidates, but the vendor owner, hiring leader, and HR each believe another party owns oversight. A policy requires review, yet operators routinely accept rankings without evidence.

The advisor identifies accountability and meaningful-review gaps, constrains expanded use, and recommends an accountability charter plus a behavior contract before autonomy design. HR leadership owns the decision; legal counsel determines jurisdiction-specific obligations; a workflow observation verifies whether review actually changes decisions.
