# Worked Example: Technician Knowledge Search

**Synthetic example.** Field technicians spend a median 14 minutes locating approved repair guidance; interviews and search logs show repeated source switching.

> If we provide citation-grounded retrieval across approved service manuals for appliance technicians, then time-to-correct-guidance will fall without increasing unsafe recommendations, because technicians can search one authoritative source layer instead of five repositories.

The economic hypothesis is more completed appointments and less escalation time. A non-AI alternative—consolidated indexed search—will be tested alongside retrieval-assisted answers. The riskiest assumptions are source rights, freshness, and whether answers remain grounded under ambiguous queries.

Next test: 30 technicians, four weeks, shadowed safety review. Continue only if time falls by at least 20%, citation support remains above the agreed threshold, and no critical safety case is answered without escalation. The service operations director owns the investment decision.
