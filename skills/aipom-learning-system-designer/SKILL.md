# AIPOM Learning System Designer

## What Is It

Design an operating system that moves role capability from instruction into applied work, feedback, coaching, peer learning, assessed progression, manager reinforcement, reusable practices, and measurable outcomes.

## Why Use It

Courses and tool demos decay when people lack practice, feedback, permission, useful workflows, and support. A learning system connects competence to the real decisions and operating conditions people must improve.

## When to Use It

Use after role competencies and priority workflows are clear. Start with a bounded role and practice where capability can be observed; do not launch an enterprise curriculum before identifying the work it must change.

## What It Produces

- Target roles, decisions, competencies, and progression
- Instruction, deliberate practice, real-work application, coaching, peer feedback, and assessment
- Manager, community, resource, and workflow reinforcement
- Accessibility, support, stewardship, evidence, and improvement cadence

## Who Should Participate

Include practitioners, role leaders, Product Operations, learning and development, managers, coaches, workflow owners, governance partners, and people affected by assessment or progression decisions.

## Evidence to Bring

Bring competency maps, work artifacts, workflow evidence, decisions, failure examples, capability assessments, support requests, manager routines, reuse evidence, barriers, and outcome baselines.

## How to Do It

1. Define the role, decisions, workflow outcomes, and capability gap.
2. Load supplied evidence and separate individual learning needs from system barriers.
3. Select a small set of observable competencies and progression evidence.
4. Design instruction only for knowledge needed before practice.
5. Create representative practice with feedback, reflection, and safe failure.
6. Integrate application into real work with coaching and peer review.
7. Define manager reinforcement, communities, office hours, examples, and reusable skills.
8. Establish accessible assessment and progression without turning the system into credential theater.
9. Measure changed behavior, decision quality, workflow outcomes, reuse, and unintended burden.
10. Assign stewardship, review cadence, and revise or retire rules.

## Facilitation Protocol

Support guided, context-dump, and best-guess modes during design. Begin with the work outcome, not course topics. Ask only questions that change the learning architecture. In best-guess mode propose a small pilot and label organizational commitments and learner needs as assumptions.

## Decision Logic

1. **Teach:** a knowledge gap blocks safe practice.
2. **Practice:** people know concepts but cannot perform representative work.
3. **Coach:** judgment varies and feedback is the missing mechanism.
4. **Reinforce:** capability exists but managers, incentives, access, or workflows suppress use.
5. **Reuse:** effective local practice should become governed and discoverable.
6. **Redesign the system:** repeated learner failure reflects authority, tool, policy, capacity, or workflow barriers.

Do not prescribe more training when evidence points to a non-learning constraint.

## Completion Criteria

Finish with role and workflow scope, competency targets, applied pathway, practice and coaching, assessment evidence, system supports, accessibility and fairness safeguards, measures, owners, pilot decision, and improvement cadence.

## Key Concepts

- Capability is demonstrated in work.
- Feedback and reflection convert activity into learning.
- Managers and workflows shape transfer more than course completion.
- Progression should increase responsible ownership, not status alone.

## Organizational Applications

Use for Product Managers, leaders, designers, engineers, data specialists, governance partners, operations, facilitators, and cross-functional product teams.

## Common Pitfalls

- Starting with a course catalog
- Giving every role identical training
- Measuring attendance and confidence only
- Assessing knowledge without applied judgment
- Blaming learners for system barriers
- Running a cohort with no ongoing owner
