# Set AI Product Direction

Use this entry path to turn broad AI ambition into product choices, non-goals, portfolio implications, and owned first commitments.

## Entry Modes

- **Guided:** begin with the product or portfolio decision leadership must make.
- **Context dump:** inspect existing strategy, research, portfolio, financial, governance, and initiative material before asking gaps.
- **Best guess:** label assumptions and propose a provisional direction plus the evidence that could change it.

## Sequence

1. Define the accountable product authority, scope, horizon, customer and business conditions, and decision required.
2. Run [`aipom-strategy-thesis-advisor`](../skills/aipom-strategy-thesis-advisor/SKILL.md) to make focus, choices, non-goals, boundaries, outcomes, and assumptions explicit.
3. Run [`aipom-portfolio-posture-advisor`](../skills/aipom-portfolio-posture-advisor/SKILL.md) to test whether current and proposed investments reflect the thesis.
4. Select the first commitments, constrained work, stopped work, evidence-producing bets, owners, and review triggers.
5. Route initiative, operating-model, governance, and technical questions to their accountable owners and focused AIPOM paths.

## Context Handoff

Preserve customer and business evidence, strategic choices, non-goals, causal assumptions, portfolio inventory, investment posture, dependencies, disagreements, constrained decisions, owners, and review triggers.

## Stop and Escalation Rules

- Do not convert executive preference, vendor momentum, or an initiative count into product strategy evidence.
- Do not manufacture consensus when product, technology, finance, and governance leaders disagree.
- Do not approve initiative readiness or specialist decisions through this organization-level direction exercise.
- Do not call every visible initiative strategic; choices require work that will not proceed.

## Completion

Finish with an evidence-aware AI product thesis, explicit choices and non-goals, portfolio implications, first commitments and constraints, named owners, unresolved authority decisions, and a dated review trigger.
