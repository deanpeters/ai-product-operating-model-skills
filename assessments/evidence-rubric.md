# Assessment Evidence Rubric

Use this rubric alongside the canonical assessment and scoring model.

## Evidence Strength

| Rating | Description | Typical signals |
|---|---|---|
| 0 — None | No inspectable evidence was supplied. | Confidence, aspiration, or unsupported recollection only |
| 1 — Anecdotal | One or more examples exist, but scope, recency, or reliability is weak. | Individual practice, single pilot, undated artifact, self-report |
| 2 — Documented | A defined artifact or policy exists with a plausible owner and scope. | Approved process, charter, standard, template, or documented decision |
| 3 — Demonstrated use | Multiple current examples show the practice being used in its intended scope. | Decision records, repeated workflow use, control operation, evaluation history |
| 4 — Demonstrated impact | Evidence shows the practice changes decisions, behavior, outcomes, risk, or reuse. | Before-and-after measures, avoided loss, improved outcomes, changed funding or launch decisions |

Evidence strength informs but does not mechanically determine maturity. A documented policy may support score 2 but cannot prove operationalized use.

## Confidence

| Confidence | Use when |
|---|---|
| Low | Evidence is absent, stale, narrow, conflicting, or mostly self-reported |
| Medium | Evidence is relevant and credible but incomplete in scope, representativeness, or outcome connection |
| High | Multiple current, relevant sources demonstrate use and consequences across the assessed scope |

Every score must include a confidence rating and short rationale.

## Scope and Representativeness

Check whether evidence represents:

- The stated operating level
- More than one team or initiative when making organization-level claims
- Leadership, practitioner, governance, and affected-user perspectives where relevant
- Normal cases and consequential failures
- The current environment rather than a superseded practice

A strong initiative example does not prove organization-level maturity.

## Evidence Record

For each assessment question, record:

```text
Question ID:
Scope and operating level:
Claim:
Evidence reviewed:
Evidence strength (0-4):
Quality limitations:
Respondent disagreement:
Maturity score (1-5):
Confidence (low/medium/high):
Critical gap (yes/no):
Decision implication:
Next evidence-producing action:
```

## Hard Rules

- Evidence strength `0` caps maturity at `2 — Emerging`.
- A document alone does not prove adoption.
- Adoption alone does not prove impact.
- Critical governance, legal, privacy, security, safety, or accountability gaps are reported separately and cannot be averaged away.
- Conflicting evidence lowers confidence until the conflict is explained or resolved.
- Missing evidence is not automatically proof of absence; it limits the supported claim.
