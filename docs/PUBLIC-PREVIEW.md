# v0.5 Public Preview Contract

## Release Position

`v0.5` is an early public preview of the complete 41-skill AIPOM library. The pre-1.0 version signals that the structure, language, facilitation behavior, interoperability, and evidence base may change as people use and critique it.

The release is built with synthetic examples and has passed structural validation, source-traceability checks, clean-checkout packaging, and isolated synthetic forward-tests. It has not completed representative organizational field validation.

## What This Release Claims

- The repository contains a coherent assessment-to-action operating-model library.
- Its canonical skills conform to the documented structure and validation rules.
- Six representative skills produced bounded, evidence-aware decisions in isolated synthetic scenarios.
- Known synthetic-test findings were incorporated and retested.
- The release packages can be reproduced from a clean checkout.

## What This Release Does Not Claim

- That the methods are field-proven or universally applicable
- That organizations have adopted them consistently
- That using a skill improves a business, customer, safety, or risk outcome
- That a maturity score, policy, workshop, or completed artifact proves operational capability
- That a skill replaces accountable specialist judgment
- That completing a skill establishes legal or regulatory compliance
- That synthetic examples represent real organizations, customers, or clients

## Feedback Priorities

The most valuable public feedback identifies:

1. A recommendation that could cause unsafe, unauthorized, or materially misleading action
2. A critical gap that disappears inside a score, value claim, or workflow
3. A question or term that creates avoidable facilitation friction
4. A missing perspective, decision authority, or evidence requirement
5. A field session where the method clarified, changed, constrained, or failed to support a decision
6. A difference between two facilitators that matters to the resulting decision

Use the repository issue forms and remove confidential, identifying, privileged, personal, contractual, or security-sensitive information before submitting.

## Path to v0.6

The `v0.6` evidence cycle will use public feedback and the three planned human pilots to strengthen examples, decision logic, facilitation, interoperability, and field evidence. Catalog growth is not the priority.
