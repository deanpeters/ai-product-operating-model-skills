# Executive Adoption Package: Quarterly AI Portfolio Review

Use this package to reallocate capital and capacity across AI initiatives and shared capabilities using strategy, outcomes, economics, readiness, production evidence, dependencies, risk, and learning.

## Sponsor Decision

> Which initiatives should scale, continue, change, pause, stop, or return to learning—and which shared capabilities deserve investment before individual bets can progress?

The package is a decision cadence, not a reporting meeting or demo parade.

## Best Fit

- The portfolio contains zombie pilots or premature scale
- Investment decisions use inconsistent evidence
- Shared context, evaluation, governance, or capability gaps block several initiatives
- Vendor, platform, data, or talent concentration affects allocation
- Leaders need to move capital and capacity rather than merely review status

## Sponsor and Participants

The accountable sponsor is the portfolio investment authority. Include product and technology leadership, Product Operations or portfolio operations, finance, data or platform leadership, governance representation, and initiative owners invited for the decisions that concern them.

## Evidence to Prepare

For each initiative, prepare a compact decision record covering:

- Strategic fit, customer outcome, and current stage
- Investment to date, forecast full cost, and economic evidence
- Readiness, production behavior, incidents, and affected-party evidence
- Platform, vendor, data, integration, talent, switching, and exit dependencies
- Current conditions, exceptions, owners, expiry, and next evidence trigger
- Requested decision and recommended portfolio posture

Also prepare shared-capability evidence and the previous quarter's decisions, conditions, and learning.

## Indicative Engagement

**Planning range:** 2–4 hour recurring decision meeting plus preparation. Initial setup may require additional work to establish comparable records and stage gates.

1. **Prepare asynchronously:** normalize evidence without flattening different initiative consequences into one score.
2. **Diagnose portfolio posture:** use the [AI Portfolio Posture Advisor](../../skills/aipom-portfolio-posture-advisor/SKILL.md) to identify imbalance, weak evidence, premature scale, vendor exposure, and zombie work.
3. **Apply investment discipline:** use [AI Investment Stage Gates](../../skills/aipom-investment-stage-gates/SKILL.md) to distinguish learning, validation, scale, pause, stop, and exceptions.
4. **Review material evidence:** draw on economic cases, dependency audits, initiative readiness, and [Production Evidence Reviews](../../skills/aipom-production-evidence-review/SKILL.md) in proportion to each decision.
5. **Make allocations:** run the [AIPOM Portfolio Quarterly Review](../../skills/aipom-portfolio-quarterly-review/SKILL.md) to decide initiative and shared-capability allocations.
6. **Record and communicate:** document rationale, conditions, owners, expiry, portfolio assumptions, and the next review agenda.

## Package Outputs

- Portfolio posture and imbalance diagnosis
- Comparable but consequence-aware initiative decision records
- Scale, continue, change, pause, stop, or return-to-learning decisions
- Capital and capacity reallocations
- Shared-capability investments and dependencies
- Exceptions, owners, expiry, and evidence conditions
- Updated strategy assumptions and next-quarter agenda

## Exit Conditions

The review is complete when allocation decisions have owners and conditions, stopped work is actually removed from active commitments, exceptions have expiry, and the next evidence and review triggers are recorded. A portfolio dashboard without reallocation is not complete.

## Authority and Caveats

The portfolio authority decides investment and capacity. Initiative, finance, legal, privacy, security, safety, risk, and governance authorities retain decisions within their remit. Strong portfolio value cannot average away a critical initiative control gap. This package does not establish organization maturity or initiative compliance.
