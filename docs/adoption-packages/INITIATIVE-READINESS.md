# Executive Adoption Package: Initiative Readiness

Use this package before a material launch, scale, sensitive-data, autonomy, market expansion, vendor change, or return-to-operation decision for one AI initiative.

## Sponsor Decision

> Given the proposed scope and consequence, may this initiative proceed now—and under what constraints, evidence conditions, ownership, expiry, and review trigger?

Readiness is specific to users, environment, data, behavior, authority, jurisdiction, and exposure. It is not a permanent badge.

## Best Fit

- Prototype-to-pilot, pilot-to-launch, or launch-to-scale gates
- Increased AI authority or automated action
- Sensitive-data use or a new population or jurisdiction
- Material model, vendor, workflow, or architecture change
- Post-incident return to operation
- Conflicting claims about product, technical, or governance readiness

## Sponsor and Participants

The accountable sponsor is the owner of the material product or operating decision. Include the Product Manager, technical and operational owners, finance where material, affected-user or domain representation, and legal, privacy, security, safety, risk, compliance, and governance specialists proportionate to consequence.

## Evidence to Prepare

- Opportunity, outcome, bet, and economic evidence
- Alternatives, dependencies, and exit exposure
- Workflow, context, source, data, and lifecycle evidence
- Behavior contract, representative evaluations, thresholds, and production signals
- Human-AI responsibilities, autonomy boundaries, accountability, and controls
- Monitoring, incident, rollback, recovery, support, adoption, and capability evidence
- Required specialist decisions and unresolved disagreements

## Indicative Engagement

**Planning range:** 4–8+ facilitated hours across preparation and 2–4 review sessions. Consequential specialist work may extend the calendar time.

1. **Define the scoped decision:** name the population, environment, data, autonomy, jurisdiction, consequence, and accountable owner.
2. **Load the evidence:** preserve what is known, assumed, missing, disputed, and awaiting specialist decision.
3. **Repair prerequisites proportionately:** use economic, dependency, context, data, behavior, evaluation, accountability, autonomy, or incident skills only where they can change the decision.
4. **Run the review:** use the [AIPOM Initiative Readiness Review](../../skills/aipom-initiative-readiness-review/SKILL.md) to profile value, dependencies, workflow, context/data, evaluation, governance/controls, and capability/operation.
5. **Make the decision:** choose proceed, proceed with constraints, remediate and re-review, pause, or stop.
6. **Record operation:** define any permitted interim motion, conditions, owners, expiry, monitoring, and next review trigger.

The [practitioner journey](../journeys/EVALUATE-AN-AI-INITIATIVE.md) provides the detailed engagement flow and smallest useful early-stage version.

## Package Outputs

- Scoped decision and evidence ledger
- Readiness profiles, confidence, disagreements, and critical gaps
- Constrained actions and required specialist decisions
- Proceed, proceed with constraints, remediate and re-review, pause, or stop recommendation
- Permitted interim motion
- Conditions, owners, expiry, monitoring, and re-review trigger

## Exit Conditions

The package is complete when the accountable decision and required specialist decisions are recorded, blocked actions remain blocked, conditions have owners and expiry, and the next review trigger is explicit. A completed checklist or overall readiness score is not sufficient.

## Authority and Caveats

The review synthesizes evidence and recommends a path. It does not approve funding, accept risk, authorize data use, certify compliance, or impersonate accountable specialists. Strong value or model performance cannot compensate for a critical legal, privacy, security, safety, accountability, evaluation, rollback, or incident-response gap.
