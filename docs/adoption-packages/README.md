# AIPOM Executive Adoption Packages

These packages organize existing AIPOM skills around recognizable executive decisions and practitioner engagements. They are curated routes through the library, not new canonical skills, certifications, or fixed consulting recipes.

Use the smallest package capable of supporting the decision. Adjust depth to consequence, evidence, operating level, and organizational capacity. Do not run every linked skill merely because it appears in a package.

## Choose a Package

| Package | Use when | Executive decision | Indicative shape |
|---|---|---|---|
| [Executive Alignment](EXECUTIVE-ALIGNMENT.md) | AI ambition is broad, fragmented, or disconnected from product strategy | Where will the organization focus, what will it avoid, and what moves first? | 3–5 facilitated hours across 2–3 sessions |
| [AI Investment Decision](AI-INVESTMENT-DECISION.md) | An opportunity needs discovery, validation, funding, continuation, or stop discipline | What level of investment is justified by current evidence? | 4–7 hours across 2–4 sessions |
| [Initiative Readiness](INITIATIVE-READINESS.md) | A material launch, scale, sensitive-data, autonomy, or return-to-operation decision is pending | May the scoped initiative proceed, and under what conditions? | 4–8+ hours across preparation and review |
| [Human-AI Workflow Pilot](HUMAN-AI-WORKFLOW-PILOT.md) | A recurring product-team decision may benefit from AI assistance | Should the redesigned motion be adopted, revised, contained, or stopped? | 4–7+ facilitated hours plus a real-use pilot |
| [Governance and Accountability](GOVERNANCE-AND-ACCOUNTABILITY.md) | Ownership, authority, controls, escalation, or incident readiness is unclear | Which actions are permitted, who decides, and what must happen when the system fails? | 5–9 hours plus an exercise where needed |
| [Quarterly AI Portfolio Review](QUARTERLY-AI-PORTFOLIO-REVIEW.md) | Leaders need to reallocate capital and capacity across AI initiatives | What should scale, continue, change, pause, stop, or receive shared-capability investment? | 2–4 hour recurring review plus preparation |

## How Packages Relate to the Library

Each package has four layers:

1. **Executive decision:** the consequential choice the sponsor must own.
2. **Engagement shape:** preparation, participants, sessions, and decision meeting.
3. **Canonical methods:** selected AIPOM skills and workflows used in proportion to the decision.
4. **Evidence and follow-through:** conditions, owners, measures, expiry, and next review.

The package guide explains the route. Canonical `SKILL.md` files remain the source of truth for method content, evidence requirements, facilitation behavior, and completion criteria.

## Selection Rules

- Start with the decision, not the package name.
- Use supplied evidence and avoid making participants repeat available context.
- Add specialist depth when consequence requires it; do not add every skill by default.
- Separate organization maturity, portfolio posture, initiative readiness, and workflow performance.
- Do not let expected value or strong scores compensate for a critical authority, safety, legal, privacy, security, evaluation, rollback, or accountability gap.
- Treat time ranges as planning guides, not guarantees.
- A package is complete when the accountable decision and next organizational motion are clear—not when every artifact is filled out.
