# Executive Adoption Package: Human-AI Workflow Pilot

Use this package to test whether AI assistance improves one recurring product-team decision or productive motion without hiding judgment, review burden, failure, or accountability.

## Sponsor Decision

> Should this redesigned human-AI motion be adopted, revised, contained, or stopped based on representative use and decision outcomes?

The package optimizes a decision cycle, not document throughput or tool adoption.

## Best Fit

- A recurring decision is slow, inconsistent, context-poor, or burdened by rework
- AI is being added to an existing workflow without redesigning responsibilities
- Apparent automation gains may be shifting work into review or exception handling
- A team wants to test a local productive motion before standardizing it
- Leaders need evidence before investing in broader workflow change

## Sponsor and Participants

The sponsor owns the workflow outcome or improvement investment. Include the decision owner, people performing and affected by the work, the Product Manager, Product Operations, design, research, engineering, data, operations, and governance partners proportionate to the proposed data and authority.

## Evidence to Prepare

- Representative observed cases, including exceptions or failures
- Trigger, inputs, sources, decisions, handoffs, delays, rework, and outcomes
- Cycle-time, quality, burden, escalation, and affected-user evidence
- Existing tools, prompts, context, and informal workarounds
- Decision rights, approvals, controls, fallback, and incident history
- Known context, data, behavior, evaluation, or capability constraints

## Indicative Engagement

**Planning range:** 4–7+ facilitated hours across multiple sessions, followed by a bounded pilot over real calendar time.

1. **Map actual work:** use the [AIPOM Productive Motion Map](../../skills/aipom-productive-motion-map/SKILL.md) to diagnose observed decisions, actors, inputs, handoffs, delays, rework, and failure.
2. **Set responsibilities:** use the [Human-AIPOM Work Contract](../../skills/human-aipom-work-contract/SKILL.md) to allocate AI assistance, human judgment, review, approval, accountability, escalation, and fallback.
3. **Add proportionate foundations:** define authoritative context, acceptable behavior, evaluation, autonomy, and accountability where consequence requires them.
4. **Design and pilot:** use [AIPOM Decision Cycle Redesign](../../skills/aipom-decision-cycle-redesign/SKILL.md) to establish the baseline, future cycle, measures, countermeasures, bounded comparison, and adoption decision.
5. **Package only when earned:** use the [AIPOM Workflow Playbook Builder](../../skills/aipom-workflow-playbook-builder/SKILL.md) after representative use supports reuse.

The [practitioner journey](../journeys/REDESIGN-A-PRODUCT-TEAM-WORKFLOW.md) provides detailed session preparation and a smaller diagnostic entry version.

## Package Outputs

- Observed productive-motion map and baseline
- Friction, failure, exception, and hidden-burden diagnosis
- Human-AI work contract and authority boundaries
- Redesigned decision cycle and bounded pilot
- Outcome, cycle, rework, burden, safety, and adoption measures
- Adopt, revise, contain, or stop decision
- Workflow owner, review cadence, and playbook when earned

## Exit Conditions

The package is complete when representative pilot evidence supports an adoption decision, responsibilities and authority are explicit, fallback works, and ownership plus review cadence are assigned. A workshop design or faster artifact generation is not sufficient.

## Authority and Caveats

The decision owner remains accountable for the workflow outcome. AI action must remain within explicit authority. Required human review needs capacity and stop authority. Specialists retain decisions within legal, privacy, security, safety, risk, and governance remits. Do not standardize an untested redesign.

## Next Motions

- Adopt and steward the workflow with outcome and burden monitoring.
- Revise and repeat the pilot when evidence is mixed.
- Contain data, actions, users, or autonomy while resolving a critical gap.
- Convert stable practice into a playbook or reusable skill only after evidence supports it.
