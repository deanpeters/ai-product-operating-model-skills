# Executive Adoption Package: Executive Alignment

Use this package to convert broad AI ambition and scattered initiatives into a bounded product strategy thesis, portfolio posture, and owned first operating motions.

## Sponsor Decision

> Where will AI support product and business strategy, what will the organization deliberately avoid, how should the current portfolio be treated, and which few operating-model conditions should move first?

This package supports strategic alignment. It is not a complete organizational maturity assessment or a substitute for initiative-level readiness decisions.

## Best Fit

- Executives use different definitions of AI value or success
- The portfolio contains disconnected pilots or premature scale
- Tool adoption has outrun customer, economic, evidence, or governance choices
- Leaders need explicit non-goals and boundaries
- A sponsor needs a credible first 90-day operating agenda

## Sponsor and Participants

The accountable sponsor is normally the CPO, CTO, business-unit leader, or jointly named product and technology executive.

Include product strategy, Product Operations, portfolio or finance, technology and data leadership, and governance representation. Bring selected product-team or customer evidence so alignment is not based only on executive aspiration.

## Evidence to Prepare

- Product and business strategy
- Customer, end-user, and workflow evidence
- Current AI initiative inventory, investment, stage, and ownership
- Existing outcome, economic, dependency, evaluation, incident, and adoption evidence
- Known legal, privacy, security, safety, risk, or governance constraints
- Examples of where stated strategy and actual portfolio behavior disagree

## Indicative Engagement

**Planning range:** 3–5 facilitated hours across 2–3 sessions, plus preparation.

1. **Orient:** define the leadership decision, scope, evidence, assumptions, and material disagreements.
2. **Choose:** use the [AI Strategy Thesis Advisor](../../skills/aipom-strategy-thesis-advisor/SKILL.md) to establish focus, outcomes, boundaries, non-goals, and next learning bets.
3. **Inspect the portfolio:** use the [AI Portfolio Posture Advisor](../../skills/aipom-portfolio-posture-advisor/SKILL.md) to identify imbalance, premature scale, vendor exposure, and zombie pilots.
4. **Select first motions:** define what to contain, learn, establish, or scale, with owners and 30- and 90-day evidence milestones.
5. **Escalate proportionately:** use the [organization assessment journey](../journeys/ASSESS-AN-ORGANIZATION.md) when leaders need a full seven-category baseline and 365-day roadmap.

Do not run a complete assessment by default. Executive alignment may reveal that one portfolio, initiative, workflow, or governance decision is the right first intervention.

## Package Outputs

- Evidence-aware AI product strategy thesis
- Strategic choices, boundaries, and non-goals
- Portfolio posture and initiative treatment recommendations
- Priority assumptions and next learning bets
- First 30- and 90-day operating motions
- Named sponsors, owners, evidence milestones, and review date
- Disagreements and unresolved specialist decisions

## Exit Conditions

The package is complete when leaders have made explicit choices, assigned the first motions, and scheduled an evidence review. A strategy narrative without portfolio implications, owners, or non-goals is not complete.

## Authority and Caveats

The sponsor owns strategic and portfolio commitments. Finance, legal, privacy, security, safety, risk, and governance authorities retain decisions within their remit. Alignment does not establish organization maturity, initiative readiness, compliance, or field-proven effectiveness.
