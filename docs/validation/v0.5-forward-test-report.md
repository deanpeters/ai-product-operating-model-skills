# v0.5 Representative Forward-Test Report

## Purpose

This report records the first Stage 3 and Stage 4 validation cycle for the `v0.5` release candidate. It tests whether six representative skills produce decision-useful outputs from incomplete context and whether their instructions expose evidence, authority, and facilitation ambiguities.

This is **synthetic forward-testing**, not external field evidence. Independent agent runs received only the target skills and raw fictional scenarios. They did not receive the expected answer, suspected defects, or planned changes. No customer, client, or confidential material was used.

## Test Matrix

| Perspective | Scenario | Skills exercised | Decision expected |
|---|---|---|---|
| Executive and operating-model leadership | B2B services company with 14 disconnected pilots, one efficiency signal, one harmful failure, and policy-practice gaps | `aipom-operating-model-assessment`; `aipom-strategy-thesis-advisor` | Provisional maturity baseline and a bounded two-quarter thesis |
| Product and initiative team | Appeal-summary assistant with narrow retrospective evidence, material omissions, sensitive data, and missing recovery controls | `aipom-use-case-triage`; `aipom-initiative-readiness-review` | Investment posture and a separate live-pilot readiness decision |
| Governance and operations | Marketplace refund agent with sparse high-value evidence, untested payout freezes, missing execution logs, and untested rollback | `aipom-autonomy-boundary-designer` | Atomic action boundaries and evidence required to expand authority |
| Portfolio leadership | Four initiatives competing for two material investments, including a threshold failure, unowned pilot, and shared evaluation dependency | `aipom-portfolio-quarterly-review` | Explicit allocation, pause, stop, exception, and shared-capability decisions |

## Evaluation Criteria

Each output was reviewed for:

1. Reuse of supplied context without unnecessary questions
2. Separation of evidence, reported claims, assumptions, and recommendations
3. Preservation of missing perspectives and specialist authority
4. Critical-gap logic before scores, value, or relative ranking
5. A usable recommendation with scope, owner, evidence condition, and next trigger
6. Clear distinction between organizational maturity, triage, initiative readiness, and autonomy
7. Facilitation instructions another agent or human could apply consistently
8. Examples that teach reasoning rather than merely show a conclusion

## Results

All six skills produced the promised class of artifact and reached an appropriate bounded decision. The tests correctly prevented narrow efficiency or forecast evidence from overriding quality, accountability, privacy, security, rollback, or authority gaps. They preserved specialist decisions and separated organization maturity from initiative readiness.

The cycle surfaced seven material instruction defects:

| Finding | Why it matters | Hardening applied |
|---|---|---|
| Missing evidence could be interpreted as `1 — Absent` | Absence of an artifact is not proof that a practice is absent | Added `Not assessed` rules to the scoring model, evidence rubric, and assessment workflow |
| Missing owners or dates invited plausible invention | A proposed role can be mistaken for accepted accountability | Shared facilitation now requires an unassigned role and event trigger rather than an invented person or date |
| Single-proposal triage lacked an explicit comparator | A proposal can look attractive without comparison to the current or non-AI workflow | Triage now requires a status-quo, non-AI, or do-nothing comparator |
| Triage and readiness could blur together | Selection for validation is not permission for live operation | Added an explicit handoff from triage to bet, gate, or readiness workflows |
| `Human approval required` did not identify who executes | Approval-token execution and human-only execution create different authority | The autonomy skill and template now record who executes after approval |
| Aggregate agreement could be mistaken for sufficient autonomy evidence | Sparse high-consequence cases can disappear inside a strong overall result | Added prospective, action-specific threshold and representativeness guidance |
| Portfolio capacity and exceptions were underspecified | Different interpretations can produce different allocations from the same evidence | Added capacity-unit, allocation-assumption, exception-authority, safeguard, and expiry rules |

## Example Hardening

The worked and weak examples for use-case triage, initiative readiness, and quarterly portfolio review were previously too short to demonstrate the complete reasoning path. They now include evidence profiles, critical gaps, authority, alternatives, decisions, conditions, and next triggers. Release validation enforces substantive worked and weak examples for all six representative skills.

## Post-Hardening Verification

Fresh scenarios verified the two most consequential cross-cutting changes. A partial assessment left unobserved categories `Not assessed` instead of manufacturing `1 — Absent` scores or category medians. A readiness review with no named business owner or launch date recommended shadow work only, kept the required role unassigned, reserved privacy and employment-counsel decisions, and used evidence completion rather than an invented date as the re-review trigger.

The complete repository validation and release build must still pass after every hardening change.

## Remaining External Field Gate

This cycle demonstrates internal behavioral coherence; it does not demonstrate human usability, actual session duration, adoption, decision impact, or independent facilitator repeatability. Before the public `v0.5` release:

- Run the representative scenarios with actual executive, product-team, and governance participants or facilitators.
- Capture time, terminology friction, unnecessary questions, missing evidence, disagreements, and decisions changed.
- Compare facilitator outputs for material divergence.
- Incorporate permitted, anonymized findings without importing client-confidential content.

The external field-validation gate remains open until that evidence exists.
