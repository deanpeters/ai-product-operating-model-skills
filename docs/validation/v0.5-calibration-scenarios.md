# v0.5 Independent-Facilitator Calibration Scenarios

Use these fictional scenarios to compare facilitation behavior without exposing real organizational material. Give the facilitator only the scenario, target skill, and relevant canonical repository files. Do not provide the prior synthetic output or expected decision.

## Calibration Rules

- Use a fresh facilitator who did not author the latest skill changes.
- Do not reveal prior findings, preferred posture, or hidden scoring notes.
- Record the output with the session capture form.
- Compare decision posture, critical gaps, authority, assumptions, and next trigger—not identical wording.
- A different posture can pass when the evidence-based reason is explicit and no consequential condition is omitted.

## Scenario A: Partial Operating-Model Assessment

**Target skill:** `aipom-operating-model-assessment`

A 400-person software company reports three AI pilots, a monthly pilot review, and a written security intake. One team reports using a quality rubric twice. No information is supplied about context lifecycle, applied learning, production evaluation, or incident response, and nobody claims those practices are absent. Leadership wants a provisional partial profile in best-guess mode without follow-up questions.

**Observe:** treatment of unobserved conditions, confidence, category arithmetic, critical unknowns, and next evidence actions.

## Scenario B: Initiative Readiness Without Assigned Authority

**Target skill:** `aipom-initiative-readiness-review`

A recruiting team wants a two-week live pilot of an AI interview-note summarizer. A 100-interview English-language shadow test reduced note cleanup by 30% and missed two accommodation requests. Humans make every hiring decision. Security review is complete. Privacy, employment counsel, and the accountable business decision owner are not identified. No launch date is supplied. Monitoring and rollback are drafted but untested.

**Observe:** live versus shadow motion, missing authority, specialist decisions, representativeness, human-review assumptions, recovery evidence, expiry, and re-review trigger.

## Scenario C: Refund-Agent Autonomy

**Target skill:** `aipom-autonomy-boundary-designer`

A retail marketplace agent may read order history and fraud flags, draft responses, issue refunds, freeze seller payouts, and refer suspected fraud. Human agents currently refund up to $250; managers approve larger refunds and all freezes. In 500 historical cases the agent agreed with senior reviewers 94%, but only 18 cases exceeded $250, no payout-freeze cases appeared, and fraud cases produced the most disagreement. Output logs exist but tool-execution logs do not. Rollback is documented but untested. Operations requests unattended refunds up to $500.

**Observe:** atomic actions, current authority, approval versus execution, action-specific evidence, logging, rollback, escalation, prohibited actions, and authority-change rules.
