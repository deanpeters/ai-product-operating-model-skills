# v0.5 Field-Validation Facilitator Runbook

## Facilitator Contract

Your job is to test whether the skill works, not rescue it invisibly. Follow the canonical skill, note where you must improvise, and preserve evidence gaps, disagreement, and human authority.

Do not teach the participants the “right answer,” manufacture consensus, supply missing organizational facts, or convert a recommended role into accepted accountability.

## Before the Session

1. Assign a session ID with no organization or participant identifiers.
2. Confirm the decision, operating level, participant mix, and permitted data boundary.
3. Store raw evidence and notes in an approved private location outside this repository.
4. Load the target skill, its template, and the relevant starting-path command.
5. Pre-populate only what the supplied evidence supports.
6. Identify missing perspectives and specialist decisions without resolving them.
7. Prepare a clean copy of the session capture form.
8. Assign a facilitator, observer, authorized decision owner if known, and timekeeper.

## Opening Script

> We are testing the method, not evaluating participants. I will reuse everything already supplied and ask only questions that could change the decision or artifact. Unknown and disagreement are valid answers. Recommendations do not replace authorized human decisions. We will record anonymized usability findings, not confidential content.

Confirm the goal and scope only if they are not already clear.

## Facilitation Pattern

Work one consequential step at a time:

1. Restate the current decision and evidence boundary.
2. Ask one targeted question only when its answer could change the diagnosis, recommendation, authority, or next action.
3. At meaningful decision points, present 3–4 numbered options with the evidence-supported recommendation first.
4. Accept combined or custom choices and synthesize them without restarting.
5. Update the context ledger after each decision.
6. Record every place where you had to interpret, skip, reorder, or supplement the skill.

Do not read empty template fields aloud or ask participants to repeat supplied information.

## Observer Protocol

The observer does not facilitate. Record timestamps and observable events:

- Repeated question
- Unexplained or disputed term
- Participant asks what decision is being made
- Evidence is treated as fact without support
- Critical gap is hidden, averaged, or deferred
- Facilitator invents an owner, approval, date, threshold, or organizational fact
- Participant disagreement is flattened
- Skill-to-skill handoff loses context
- Participant can or cannot restate the decision and next action
- Facilitator departs from the canonical instructions

Record the event and decision consequence, not a judgment about the participant.

## Skill-Specific Checks

### Executive and Portfolio Pilot

- Keep `Not assessed` separate from `1 — Absent`.
- Do not create an overall maturity score when scopes differ.
- Require the thesis to make choices and non-goals.
- Define portfolio capacity units and exception authority before allocation.
- Confirm stopped work actually releases or redirects capacity.

### Product and Initiative Pilot

- Compare the proposal with the current, non-AI, or do-nothing alternative.
- Keep `validate` separate from live-pilot readiness.
- Name any permitted lower-exposure interim motion.
- Preserve privacy, security, legal, domain, risk, and other specialist decisions.
- Require monitoring, expiry, stop conditions, and re-review triggers for constraints.

### Governance and Operations Pilot

- Split the system into atomic actions.
- Distinguish recommendation, approval, AI execution after approval, human execution only, and prohibition.
- Test reviewer competence, information, time, authority, and fallback.
- Use action-specific evidence and thresholds; do not rely on aggregate agreement.
- Name who may expand, reduce, suspend, and resume authority.

## Closing Script

Ask one question at a time:

1. “What decision became clearer or changed because of this session?”
2. “Which question, term, or step created avoidable friction?”
3. “What evidence or perspective was missing?”
4. “Please restate the next action, required owner or role, and trigger in your own words.”

Then distinguish:

- Decision actually made
- Recommendation only
- Specialist decisions still required
- Assumptions still in use
- Next action and evidence trigger

Do not close with a generic invitation to continue.

## After the Session

1. Complete the capture form while observations are fresh.
2. Remove names and identifying details.
3. Classify each finding P0–P3 using the field-validation plan.
4. Add only sanitized findings to the repository register.
5. Link accepted findings to the exact canonical file that should change.
6. Re-test any P0–P2 fix with a fresh scenario or independent facilitator.
7. Delete or retain raw material according to the approved research policy.
