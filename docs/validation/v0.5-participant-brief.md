# Participant Brief: AIPOM v0.5 Validation

## Why You Are Participating

You are helping test whether an AI Product Operating Model skill supports a real decision clearly, responsibly, and without requiring hidden expertise. The facilitator is testing the method, not you or your organization.

## What to Expect

- The facilitator will begin with the decision and supplied context.
- You may say “unknown,” disagree, or decline to answer.
- The group will distinguish evidence, reported claims, assumptions, and recommendations.
- The AI or facilitator may recommend; authorized humans retain decisions and accountability.
- The session should end with a usable artifact, unresolved questions, and a named next motion.

## What to Bring

Bring only material you are authorized to share. Useful inputs include recent decisions, evidence of actual use, counterexamples, outcome or workflow measures, evaluation results, ownership and approval records, and known gaps.

Do not bring client-confidential information, personal data, credentials, security-sensitive details, privileged legal advice, or proprietary artifacts unless the session environment and participants are explicitly approved for them.

## How Findings Are Used

Raw notes remain outside the public repository in an approved private location. Only anonymized findings and permitted patterns may enter the repository. Session participation is not evidence of organizational maturity, compliance, or endorsement.

At the end, you will be asked:

1. What decision became clearer or changed?
2. Which question or term created avoidable friction?
3. What important evidence or perspective was missing?
4. Could you explain and repeat the next action without the facilitator?
