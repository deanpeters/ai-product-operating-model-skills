# v0.5 External Field-Validation Plan

## Objective

Determine whether representative humans can use the six release-candidate skills to make clearer, evidence-aware decisions without hidden facilitation knowledge. This plan measures usability, decision usefulness, authority preservation, and repeatability. It does not attempt to prove organization-wide adoption or business impact.

## Three Pilots

Each pilot is a small research cycle and may contain more than one facilitated session. Do not force two substantial skills into one rushed workshop merely to reduce calendar count.

### Pilot A: Executive and Portfolio Leadership

**Skills:** `aipom-operating-model-assessment`, `aipom-strategy-thesis-advisor`, and `aipom-portfolio-quarterly-review`.

**Participants:** 5–8 people spanning product or technology leadership, Product Operations, finance or portfolio leadership, one practitioner, and one governance partner.

**Suggested format:**

- 30–45 minutes of private evidence preparation
- 120 minutes for a bounded assessment and strategy-thesis decision
- 75–90 minutes, separately, for a portfolio allocation decision

**Minimum evidence:** strategy choices, initiative inventory, two recent decision records, one example of demonstrated use, one counterexample or failure, current capacity constraints, and named or missing decision authorities.

**Decisions to test:** whether participants can identify the weakest consequential condition, choose a bounded two-quarter thesis, and make explicit continue, constrain, pause, stop, or shared-capability allocations.

### Pilot B: Product and Initiative Team

**Skills:** `aipom-use-case-triage` and `aipom-initiative-readiness-review`.

**Participants:** 5–8 people spanning the Product Manager, engineering or data, operations or domain expertise, an affected-user perspective, finance where material, and relevant governance specialists.

**Suggested format:**

- 60–75 minutes for triage against the current or non-AI alternative
- 90–120 minutes, separately if needed, for the readiness decision

**Minimum evidence:** problem and outcome evidence, current-workflow baseline, evaluation results, intended population, data and vendor facts, workflow and human-review design, accountability, monitoring, incident, fallback, and rollback status.

**Decisions to test:** whether participants distinguish `validate` from permission for live operation and whether the readiness review produces a scoped decision, permitted interim motion, specialist decision register, and re-review trigger.

### Pilot C: Governance and Operations

**Skill:** `aipom-autonomy-boundary-designer`.

**Participants:** 4–7 people spanning the operational owner, Product Manager, engineering, frontline reviewer or operator, and risk, legal, privacy, security, or governance roles proportionate to consequence.

**Suggested format:** 75–90 minutes plus a 20-minute participant debrief.

**Minimum evidence:** atomic proposed actions, current human authority, affected parties, representative evaluation, permissions, action and execution logs, reviewer capacity, escalation, fallback, rollback, and incident evidence.

**Decision to test:** whether participants can distinguish recommendation, approval, execution, prohibition, accountability, and the evidence required to expand or reduce autonomy.

## Recruitment and Independence

- Include at least one person who did not help author the skill or prepare the evidence package.
- Include a practitioner or affected-workflow perspective; leadership-only sessions are insufficient.
- Record missing perspectives rather than treating attendance as consensus.
- For repeatability, have a second facilitator independently run one calibration scenario for the assessment, readiness, and autonomy workflows.
- Do not brief participants on the desired decision. The purpose is to test the method, not agreement with the library.

## Success Evidence

Record both observed behavior and participant judgment.

| Dimension | Evidence to capture | Passing signal |
|---|---|---|
| Completion | Promised artifact fields and decision produced | No required decision element omitted |
| Context reuse | Repeated or unnecessary questions | No material supplied fact is requested again without reason |
| Evidence discipline | Claims, assumptions, counterevidence, and confidence | Participants can identify what is known and what remains assumed |
| Critical-gap integrity | Treatment of legal, privacy, security, safety, evaluation, accountability, or recovery gaps | No critical gap disappears inside a score or value claim |
| Authority integrity | Recommendation, specialist decisions, decision owner, and accountability | No authority or approval is invented or silently transferred |
| Decision usefulness | Decision changed, clarified, constrained, or explicitly deferred | Participants can state the decision, why, owner or required role, and next trigger |
| Terminology | Terms that require explanation or produce conflicting interpretations | No unresolved term changes the resulting decision |
| Burden | Preparation time, live time, rework, and abandoned steps | Burden is proportionate to the consequence and decision |
| Repeatability | Independent facilitator output | Same decision posture, or a traceable evidence-based reason for divergence; no consequential gap omitted |

## Severity and Release Rules

Classify findings by consequence:

- **P0 — authority or safety failure:** the method permits consequential action without required evidence, authority, or control; hides a critical gap; or creates a materially unsafe recommendation.
- **P1 — decision failure:** the method cannot produce its promised decision, conflates operating levels or stages, or omits a required owner, condition, or next trigger.
- **P2 — facilitation failure:** unnecessary questions, confusing terminology, poor handoff, or avoidable burden materially weakens the session.
- **P3 — editorial improvement:** clarity or consistency issue that does not change the decision.

The external gate closes only when:

1. All six representative skills have been used with their target participant mix.
2. The assessment, readiness, and autonomy skills each pass one independent-facilitator calibration.
3. Every session produces the promised artifact and named next motion.
4. No unresolved P0 or P1 finding remains.
5. Accepted P2 findings are fixed or explicitly deferred with rationale.
6. Sanitized findings and resulting changes are recorded in the repository.
7. The full validation, clean-clone build, archive, checksum, confidentiality, and source checks pass.

## Privacy and Research Boundaries

- Obtain participant agreement before recording or retaining raw notes.
- Use session IDs instead of participant or organization names in repository artifacts.
- Do not paste confidential source material into an AI system without authorization.
- Remove personal, client, company, product, security, legal, and contractual identifiers before committing findings.
- Treat participant claims as reported evidence unless artifacts or observation support them.
- Participants may decline questions or request that an observation not be retained.
- Completing a session does not establish compliance, maturity, adoption, or impact.
