# v0.6 Evidence Status

This page states what the `v0.6` public preview has and has not demonstrated.

| Evidence layer | Status | What that means |
|---|---|---|
| Skill structure and metadata | Complete | All 41 skills conform to the repository specification and resolve to stable assessment IDs and catalog metadata |
| Templates and examples | Complete | Every skill includes a template, synthetic worked example, and synthetic weak example |
| Internal links | Complete | Repository-relative Markdown links pass automated checking |
| Source traceability | Complete for cited skills | Frontmatter source URLs are HTTPS and appear in each skill’s Sources section |
| Synthetic forward-testing | Complete for six representative skills | Isolated scenarios tested assessment, strategy, triage, autonomy, readiness, and portfolio behavior; findings were incorporated and retested |
| Clean-checkout packaging | Complete | Canonical and Codex-compatible archives build and pass integrity and checksum verification |
| Human field validation | Not yet completed | Representative organizational participants have not yet completed the planned sessions |
| Independent human facilitator repeatability | Not yet completed | Synthetic agent retesting exists; human-facilitator comparison remains planned |
| Demonstrated organizational adoption | Not established | Repository use, completion, or download is not proof of changed practice |
| Demonstrated decision or business impact | Not established | No public claim is made that the library has improved outcomes, economics, safety, or risk posture in an organization |
| Legal or regulatory compliance | Not established | The library is not legal advice, certification, approval, or proof of compliance |

## Appropriate v0.6 Use

- Explore and critique the operating-model methods
- Run synthetic or bounded internal exercises
- Adapt templates for authorized contexts
- Compare recommendations with accountable human judgment
- Report facilitation friction, unsafe recommendations, missing evidence, or field-session findings

## Required Caution

For consequential decisions, involve the accountable business, product, technical, operational, legal, privacy, security, safety, risk, compliance, finance, or domain specialists appropriate to the context. Do not delegate their authority to the library or an AI facilitator.

The next evidence target is representative human use, independent-facilitator repeatability, anonymized field findings, and evidence that the methods clarify or change real decisions.
