# AIPOM for Product Operations

## What Is in It for You

AIPOM gives Product Operations a working system for turning scattered AI initiatives, informal team practices, and policy requirements into repeatable product decisions with evidence, ownership, and review cadence.

It helps you:

- Diagnose where the operating model is failing without turning the assessment into score theater
- Connect leadership intent to actual product-team practice
- Route teams to the smallest useful intervention
- Preserve evidence, decisions, assumptions, owners, and unresolved questions across sessions
- Establish repeatable workflows without standardizing them before they work
- Run portfolio, adoption, and operating-model review cadences
- Turn effective local practice into reusable organizational capability

Product Operations stewards the system. It does not automatically own product decisions, technical implementation, specialist approvals, or accepted risk.

## Your Plain-English Flow

### 1. Find the operating friction

Start with the decision or recurring condition that is failing. Use the [operating-model assessment](../../skills/aipom-operating-model-assessment/SKILL.md) when the problem spans the organization, the [workflow opportunity advisor](../../skills/aipom-workflow-opportunity-advisor/SKILL.md) when teams do not know which motion to improve, or the [portfolio posture advisor](../../skills/aipom-portfolio-posture-advisor/SKILL.md) when investment is scattered.

**Your job:** define scope, gather multiple perspectives, and keep missing evidence and disagreement visible.

### 2. Convene the evidence and owners

Bring the people who perform the work, own the decision, provide technical and customer evidence, and hold relevant specialist authority. Reuse supplied artifacts. Do not make teams repeat context because the work moved between skills.

Use the [accountability charter](../../skills/aipom-accountability-charter/SKILL.md) when ownership is vague and the [authoritative source map](../../skills/authoritative-source-map/SKILL.md) when teams disagree about which information should guide a decision.

**Your job:** make the decision, evidence, owner, contributors, escalation, and next review explicit.

### 3. Design the operating motion

Use the [productive motion map](../../skills/aipom-productive-motion-map/SKILL.md) to observe actual work, the [human-AIPOM work contract](../../skills/human-aipom-work-contract/SKILL.md) to divide responsibilities, and [decision-cycle redesign](../../skills/aipom-decision-cycle-redesign/SKILL.md) to test a better way of working.

**Your job:** protect the decision outcome, reveal hidden review burden, and prevent tool-first automation.

### 4. Establish a cadence

Use the [operating-model roadmap](../../skills/aipom-operating-model-roadmap/SKILL.md) for sequenced change, the [initiative readiness review](../../skills/aipom-initiative-readiness-review/SKILL.md) for a material product decision, and the [quarterly portfolio review](../../skills/aipom-portfolio-quarterly-review/SKILL.md) for recurring allocation.

**Your job:** ensure conditions have owners, expiry, evidence milestones, and decision triggers—not only due dates for documents.

### 5. Bottle and improve what works

Use the [workflow playbook builder](../../skills/aipom-workflow-playbook-builder/SKILL.md) and [workflow-to-skill converter](../../skills/workflow-to-skill-converter/SKILL.md) only after representative use. Use the [adoption impact scorecard](../../skills/aipom-adoption-impact-scorecard/SKILL.md) and [operating-model retrospective](../../skills/aipom-operating-model-retrospective/SKILL.md) to decide what to improve, reuse, constrain, or retire.

**Your job:** make effective practice travel with its context, judgment, controls, exceptions, and stewardship.

## You Are Done When

The relevant decision or practice has evidence, accountable ownership, an explicit next motion, and a review trigger. A completed workshop, roadmap, or playbook is not sufficient by itself.
