# AIPOM for Heads of Product

This persona includes Chief Product Officers, SVPs of Product, VPs of Product acting as the senior product authority, and equivalent product leadership roles.

## What Is in It for You

AIPOM helps you turn broad AI ambition into product choices, portfolio commitments, and an operating direction that teams can actually use.

It helps you:

- Decide where AI belongs in product strategy and where it does not
- Connect AI investment to customer, product, economic, and risk outcomes
- Expose competing leadership assumptions before they become scattered initiatives
- Distinguish learning bets from scaling commitments and zombie pilots
- Set explicit non-goals, constraints, owners, and evidence expectations
- Sponsor operating-model improvements without absorbing Product Operations work
- Change direction when portfolio evidence, incidents, or market conditions require it

The Head of Product owns product direction and product-portfolio commitments within the authority granted by the organization. The role does not replace the CTO's technical authority, finance's investment authority, or specialist legal, privacy, security, safety, risk, and governance decisions.

## Your Plain-English Flow

### 1. Name the decision, not the AI ambition

Begin with the product, portfolio, or organizational choice leadership must make. Define the customer and business conditions that matter, the time horizon, and the consequence of continuing without a decision.

**Your job:** turn “we need an AI strategy” into a bounded decision with an accountable owner.

### 2. Choose where AI belongs—and where it does not

Use the [strategy thesis advisor](../../skills/aipom-strategy-thesis-advisor/SKILL.md) to define focus, choices, non-goals, boundaries, outcome hypotheses, and the next learning bets.

**Your job:** make tradeoffs visible enough to guide investment and product-team decisions.

### 3. Inspect the portfolio behind the story

Use the [portfolio posture advisor](../../skills/aipom-portfolio-posture-advisor/SKILL.md) to distinguish exploration, validation, scaling, enabling capability, containment, and work that should stop.

**Your job:** ensure the portfolio reflects the strategy rather than the visibility of individual pilots or vendors.

### 4. Make the first commitments explicit

Record what moves now, what remains bounded, what evidence is required next, who owns each action, and when leadership will review or revise the direction.

**Your job:** leave teams with usable choices, not a slogan or transformation theater.

### 5. Revisit direction when evidence changes

Use the [quarterly portfolio review](../../skills/aipom-portfolio-quarterly-review/SKILL.md) when recurring capital and capacity decisions are needed. Use the [operating-model roadmap](../../skills/aipom-operating-model-roadmap/SKILL.md) after an evidence-based organizational diagnosis identifies conditions that must change.

**Your job:** treat changed choices as learning when the evidence supports the change.

## You Are Done When

Leadership has made explicit product choices and non-goals, the portfolio implications are visible, the first commitments and constrained work have owners, assumptions and disagreements remain visible, and a dated evidence-based review trigger exists.
