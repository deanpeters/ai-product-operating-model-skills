# AIPOM for Team Leads

## What Is in It for You

A Team Lead is the person accountable for coordinating the day-to-day delivery and operating health of a product team or specialist team. The title may be product, engineering, design, research, data, operations, or another cross-functional lead.

AIPOM helps Team Leads turn broad AI direction into work the team can actually perform, inspect, stop, and improve.

It helps you:

- Convert an initiative or workflow decision into clear team responsibilities
- Surface hidden handoffs, rework, review burden, and failure modes
- Establish the context, data, behavior, and evaluation foundations the team needs
- Define where AI assists, where people decide, and who can stop the work
- Run bounded pilots before standardizing or increasing autonomy
- Prepare for incidents, fallback, and recovery
- Improve or retire practices using evidence from real operation

Team Leads coordinate execution. They do not automatically own product investment, accepted risk, data authorization, legal approval, or every specialist decision.

## Your Plain-English Flow

### 1. Choose the decision or motion

Start with one recurring decision, product behavior, or initiative condition that must improve. Use the [workflow opportunity advisor](../../skills/aipom-workflow-opportunity-advisor/SKILL.md) when the priority is unclear or [opportunity framing](../../skills/aipom-opportunity-framing/SKILL.md) when the team is still defining the problem.

**Your job:** give the team a bounded outcome and stop it from beginning with a tool feature.

### 2. Observe how the work really happens

Use the [productive motion map](../../skills/aipom-productive-motion-map/SKILL.md) against representative cases. Include people who perform the work, receive it, review it, and handle exceptions.

**Your job:** establish the baseline and expose delay, rework, hidden labor, missing perspectives, and failure behavior.

### 3. Prepare what the team needs to execute

Use the [authoritative source map](../../skills/authoritative-source-map/SKILL.md), [data readiness audit](../../skills/aipom-data-readiness-audit/SKILL.md), [context package builder](../../skills/aipom-context-package-builder/SKILL.md), [behavior contract](../../skills/aipom-behavior-contract-builder/SKILL.md), and [evaluation strategy](../../skills/aipom-evaluation-strategy-advisor/SKILL.md) in proportion to the work.

**Your job:** ensure the team knows what evidence and context to trust, what acceptable behavior means, and how results will affect a decision.

### 4. Assign responsibilities and authority

Use the [human-AIPOM work contract](../../skills/human-aipom-work-contract/SKILL.md), [accountability charter](../../skills/aipom-accountability-charter/SKILL.md), and [autonomy boundary designer](../../skills/aipom-autonomy-boundary-designer/SKILL.md).

**Your job:** make preparation, recommendation, action, monitoring, human review, approval, escalation, fallback, and stop authority explicit.

### 5. Pilot the work

Use [decision-cycle redesign](../../skills/aipom-decision-cycle-redesign/SKILL.md) to compare the proposed motion with the baseline using representative cases.

**Your job:** protect scope, manual fallback, review capacity, measurement, and honest reporting of failures or burden.

### 6. Operate and respond

Use the [production evidence review](../../skills/aipom-production-evidence-review/SKILL.md) and [risk-control incident playbook](../../skills/aipom-risk-control-incident-playbook/SKILL.md) to decide whether the work continues, changes, is constrained, rolls back, or stops.

**Your job:** make escalation and recovery real operating behavior rather than policy text.

### 7. Improve or bottle the practice

Use the [workflow playbook builder](../../skills/aipom-workflow-playbook-builder/SKILL.md) only after representative use. Use the [adoption impact scorecard](../../skills/aipom-adoption-impact-scorecard/SKILL.md) to inspect whether the practice changed decisions or outcomes without unacceptable burden.

**Your job:** preserve exceptions, controls, measures, ownership, and retirement rules when the practice travels.

## You Are Done When

The team can perform the bounded work with trusted inputs, explicit responsibilities, usable review and fallback, measurable evidence, accountable decisions, and a clear path to improve, constrain, or stop.
