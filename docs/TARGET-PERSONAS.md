# AIPOM Target Personas

AIPOM serves people who sponsor decisions, perform product work, lead teams, operate AI-enabled systems, provide specialist judgment, and improve the organizational system around them.

The leadership personas are:

1. **Head of Product** — including CPOs, SVPs of Product, and equivalent senior product authorities who set product direction and product-portfolio commitments.
2. **CTO** — including equivalent senior technology executives who own technical readiness, dependencies, reliability, recovery, and technical constraints.

The execution personas are:

1. **Product Operations** — designs and stewards the operating system around product decisions and repeatable practices.
2. **Product Manager** — leads an AI opportunity from problem evidence through product decisions, operation, and learning.
3. **Team Lead** — turns decisions into coordinated team execution and keeps the work operable, reviewable, and improvable.

Finance, engineering, design, research, data, operations, legal, privacy, security, safety, risk, governance, enablement, and affected-user representatives remain essential participants. The five target personas do not absorb their authority or specialist work.

## How Persona Coverage Works

The `audience` field in each canonical skill identifies roles likely to use, facilitate, participate in, or act on that method. It does not imply that every listed role has the same authority.

Persona documentation provides a practical route through related skills. It does not create separate persona-specific versions of the methods. Canonical `SKILL.md` files remain the source of truth.

Use the generated [Skills by Target Persona](../catalog/skills-by-persona.md) index to inspect broad role relevance across the library. This is different from [Starter Pack membership](../catalog/skills-by-starter-pack.md), which identifies the bounded methods packaged for one persona-and-job combination. Head of Product coverage includes existing `CPO` and `VP Product` audience metadata; CTO coverage uses the existing `CTO` metadata.

Coverage should remain selective:

- Add a persona when the skill supports work that role performs, coordinates, decides, or operationalizes.
- Do not add every persona to every skill merely to increase coverage.
- State where specialists or executives retain decision authority.
- Connect the persona to the outcome and next motion, not only an artifact.
- Use plain language before exposing catalog structure or metadata.

## Supporting and Decision Roles

- **Executives and portfolio authorities** sponsor strategy, investment, risk posture, and organizational commitments.
- **Design, research, engineering, data, and operations practitioners** perform specialist product and operating work.
- **Legal, privacy, security, safety, risk, finance, and AI governance specialists** make decisions within their authority and define material constraints.
- **Facilitators, consultants, and trainers** help groups use the methods without taking ownership of their decisions.
- **Contributors and agent integrators** maintain and adapt the library while preserving its evidence and accountability contracts.

The intended operating pattern is shared work with explicit authority: the Head of Product sets product direction, the CTO owns technical readiness, Product Operations stewards repeatability, Product Managers lead initiative decisions, Team Leads coordinate execution, and specialists retain accountable judgment.
